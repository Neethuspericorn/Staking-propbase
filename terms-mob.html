THE PROPBASE PROTOCOL

The Propbase Protocol is a decentralized protocol on various public blockchains, including but not limited to Aptos, that allows users to trade certain compatible digital assets (the "Propbase Protocol" or the "Protocol"). The Protocol currently has one version, designated as v0.9, which comprises open-source or source-available self-executing smart contracts deployed on various public blockchains, such as Aptos. Propbase does not control or operate any version of the Protocol on any blockchain network.

The Protocol is initially deployed on the Aptos blockchain and will be deployed on several other blockchain networks, including by parties other than Propbase. Deployments on other networks typically make use of cross-chain bridges, which allow assets native to one blockchain to be transferred to another blockchain. Please note that digital assets that have been "bridged" or "wrapped" to operate on other blockchain networks (including to blockchains compatible with the Move Virtual Machine (Aptos)) are distinct from the original Aptos mainnet asset.

As a general matter, Propbase is not a liquidity provider into Protocol liquidity pools, and liquidity providers are independent third parties

4. REAL ESTATE SALES PROCESS

Propbase functions as a marketplace that facilitates connections between buyers (token holders) and sellers (individuals wishing to sell their properties). The Platform advertises real estate properties but does not engage in the purchase, flipping, or resale of properties on the Website.

The standard procedure for selling real estate on the Platform comprises the following steps:

  • Property Submission: Sellers submit their properties on the Propbase marketplace for consideration. The property undergoes an evaluation and may be approved or denied based on various factors, including occupancy, condition, location, rental history, and other pertinent criteria.
  • Property Tokenization: Upon approval, the property is placed under contract by a newly formed LLC, effectively tokenizing the property. Property Tokenization involves creating an on-chain legal entity using smart contracts and issuing digital tokens that represent shares in the created entity, thereby allowing token holders to participate in governance through a specific property DAO.
  • Property Inspection: A professional property inspection company conducts a comprehensive inspection and provides a detailed report on the property's condition. If repairs are necessary, the seller must either complete the repairs before closing or provide a credit to the LLC. The inspection covers both interior and exterior elements, including the foundation, structural components, roof, plumbing, and electrical systems.
  • Listing on Marketplace: The tokenized property is then listed on the Propbase marketplace. This process involves executing an agreement to sell the property on the marketplace, thereby making it available for purchase by prospective buyers.

As the provider of the Services, Propbase does not own, control, offer, or manage any real estate. Propbase is not a party to the contracts directly entered into between Users and does not act as a real estate broker or insurer. The Company does not serve as an agent in any capacity for any User.
